# Code of Conduct

*Tiếng Việt below · English first.*

HumanArchive's Code of Conduct is not generic. It derives directly from
the **5 immutable principles** that govern the project itself. Applying
those principles to how we treat each other as contributors.

## The 5 principles, applied to community

### 1. Don't pass judgment
Disagree with ideas freely. Don't attack people. Never claim a
contributor is "lying" — their memory is their experience. In reviews,
say "I saw X differently" instead of "you're wrong".

### 2. Don't identify others
Never doxx a contributor. Never deanonymize by cross-referencing
details. Never demand someone reveal their real name to contribute,
review, or translate. If you accidentally learn an identity, don't
share it.

### 3. Empathy before analysis
Many memories in this archive describe trauma. Contributors who bring
them here are doing something emotionally costly. Before technical
critique of their submission (schema issue, PII slip), acknowledge
what they did. Same when reviewing code from first-time contributors.

### 4. Motivation matters more than action
Someone proposing a controversial change — pause and understand *why*
before rejecting. Someone making a technical mistake — understand what
they were trying to achieve. Assume good faith by default.

### 5. Immutability — no quiet erasure
Don't delete comments, issues, or PRs because they're inconvenient.
Lock them if needed, but the record stays. This is how we keep
ourselves honest: we can't rewrite our own history, the same way the
archive can't rewrite the memories inside it.

## Concrete behaviors

### Expected

- Use the contributor's chosen handle / pronouns. Ask if unsure.
- Write disagreements in terms of the work, not the person.
- Translate or mirror contributions from non-English speakers with
  care; don't silently "fix" their voice.
- When flagging possible PII or ethics issues in a PR, do it with a
  calm, specific comment — not a public callout.
- Default to Vietnamese and English in issues/discussions; but other
  languages welcome.

### Not acceptable

- Personal attacks, slurs, discriminatory language of any kind
- Attempting to identify an anonymous contributor
- Dismissing someone's memory as "not credible" based on their role or
  background (witness vs authority vs victim — all equal)
- Brigading, vote manipulation, sockpuppeting
- Publishing private communication without consent (even if "for
  transparency")
- Using content from the archive to harass, retaliate, or intimidate
  — this violates both the CC-BY-SA content license and this Code
- Pressuring someone to reveal why they withdrew a memory

### Gray areas we discuss rather than rule

- When is a dispute annotation appropriate vs. an issue?
- Is a historical public figure (politician, general) eligible for
  pseudonymization requests? (Default: public-figure exception applies
  to their **public** actions but not private life.)
- When does curation become editorial power?

These are discussed in the open. Decisions are recorded in `docs/`.

## Enforcement

The project has no single "moderator" — by design, same as the archive
itself. Instead:

1. **Raise concerns** openly in the relevant issue/PR, or privately
   via the security contact (see `SECURITY.md`).
2. **Community response**: existing curators (those listed in
   `trust/reviewers.json`) deliberate. Quorum of 2 for minor issues,
   majority for serious ones.
3. **Graduated response**: clarify → warn → temporary review-only mode
   → instance-level ban (the person can still fork; we can't erase
   them from federation).

We apply these principles to ourselves first. If you observe a curator
violating them, call it out — no one is exempt.

## Scope

Applies to:
- GitHub repo (issues, PRs, discussions, code comments)
- Official Matrix/Discord channels (when established)
- Project events, talks, workshops
- Interactions referencing this project in other venues when acting as
  a project member

Does not apply to:
- Your behavior in unrelated spaces
- Your private views

## Tiếng Việt

Code of Conduct của HumanArchive không generic — nó bắt nguồn trực
tiếp từ 5 nguyên tắc bất biến của dự án, áp dụng vào cách chúng ta
đối xử với nhau.

**5 nguyên tắc áp dụng lên cộng đồng:**

1. **Không phán xét**: tranh luận ý tưởng, không công kích người.
2. **Không định danh**: không doxx, không ép ai khai danh.
3. **Đồng cảm trước**: nhiều ký ức ở đây là trauma — người đóng góp
   làm điều khó khăn. Ghi nhận trước khi phê bình kỹ thuật.
4. **Hiểu động cơ**: ai đề xuất thay đổi gây tranh cãi — hiểu lý do
   trước khi reject.
5. **Không xoá lặng lẽ**: không xoá comment/issue/PR vì bất tiện.
   Lock nếu cần, record phải được giữ.

**Không chấp nhận**: công kích cá nhân, cố gắng định danh người đóng
góp nặc danh, dùng archive để harass/trả đũa, brigading, sockpuppet,
công bố tin nhắn riêng tư mà không xin phép.

**Enforcement**: không có single moderator. Curator trong
`trust/reviewers.json` thảo luận và quyết định cùng nhau (quorum 2
cho vấn đề nhỏ, majority cho nghiêm trọng). Graduated response:
làm rõ → cảnh báo → review-only → instance ban (không thể ép người
khác khỏi federation — đây là giới hạn của thiết kế, không phải lỗi).
